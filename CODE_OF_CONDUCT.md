# Code of Conduct

This project follows the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/), with one practical note appended below.

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in Synapse a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people.
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences.
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback.
- Accepting responsibility, apologising to those affected by mistakes, and learning from the experience.
- Focusing on what is best for the overall community.

Examples of unacceptable behaviour:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind.
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or email address, without their explicit permission.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces (issues, PRs, discussions, security reports) and when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Reports of unacceptable behaviour can be sent to `jaisogani183@gmail.com` with subject prefix `[synapse-coc]`. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

Reporters' identity and the details of the report will be kept confidential.

## Enforcement guidelines

Project maintainers will follow the [Contributor Covenant Enforcement Ladder](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/#enforcement-guidelines): Correction → Warning → Temporary Ban → Permanent Ban.

## Practical note (Synapse-specific)

This project is small and the maintainer pool is small. We take this seriously, but we also want to keep the noise floor low. Two practical asks:

1. **Be specific.** "This is broken" is harder to act on than "X does Y, I expected Z."
2. **Be patient.** Single-maintainer projects can take a few days to respond. Assume good faith.
